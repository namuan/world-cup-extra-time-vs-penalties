Men's FIFA World Cup: full extra time vs penalty shootouts

An analysis of every knockout match that reached extra time across nine World Cups — and whether it was decided in the additional 30 minutes or went to penalties.

46 matches analysed 9 tournaments 1982–2022

Short answer

Of the 46 knockout matches in men's World Cups that reached extra time across our sample, 16 (34.8%) were decided in extra time while 30 (65.2%) went to penalties. Nearly two-thirds of all extra-time knockout matches ended in a penalty shootout rather than being settled during the additional 30 minutes.

Methodology

Step 1 — Identify candidate matches

All knockout-stage matches in the included tournaments were reviewed.

Step 2 — Filter for extra time

Only matches that were tied after 90 minutes (regulation) were retained.

Step 3 — Classify outcome

Each match was classified as either extra_time (a goal was scored during the 30-minute extra time period) or penalties (the match remained tied after extra time and proceeded to a penalty shootout).

Step 4 — Validate

Data was cross-checked against Wikipedia and all rows were programmatically validated (see tests/test_dataset.py).

The quarter-final paradox

The most striking finding emerges when you break the data down by knockout stage. While the round of 16 is relatively balanced — nearly half of extra-time matches are decided before penalties — the quarter-finals tell a completely different story. An astonishing 12 of 13 (92.3%) extra-time quarter-finals went to a shootout.

Why do quarter-finals behave so differently? The gap in quality between teams is narrower than in the round of 16, and the stakes — a semi-final spot — make both sides cautious. Teams may prefer to defend deep and trust their luck from the spot rather than risk pushing forward and conceding.

Tournament trends

The balance between extra-time goals and penalty shootouts has shifted noticeably over time. Early tournaments were more balanced — 1990 and 2014 each saw a 50/50 split across 8 matches. But recent World Cups have leaned increasingly toward shootouts, culminating in 2022 where all five extra-time matches went to penalties without a single goal in the additional 30 minutes.

Limitations

  • Manual curation: The dataset was compiled manually from RSSSF pages; some encoding issues exist for older tournaments (1982–1994).
  • Third-place matches: One third-place match (1986, France vs Belgium) reached extra time and is included in the dataset.
  • Cross-check: A full cross-check against FIFA's own records has not yet been completed.
  • Small sample: With 46 matches spread across 9 tournaments, per-tournament and per-stage breakdowns should be interpreted cautiously.
